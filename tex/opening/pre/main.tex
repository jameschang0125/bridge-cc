\section{preempts}

In this section, we shall discuss the range and responses to opening preempts or direct jump overcalls.
Here are two main "dimensions" to properly describe a preemptive hand:

\begin{itemize}
    \setlength{\itemsep}{0pt}
    \item defensiveness: the expected tricks when defending, for example, general HCP
    \item offensiveness: the expected tricks when preempting, for example, shape and KQJT in long suit
\end{itemize}

In general, a preemptive range can be defined as a subset on the defensiveness-offensiveness plane.

\subsection{2-3-4 rule revisited}

The 2-3-4 rule is a good starting point to discuss. I suggest the following modification according to vulnerability and seats:

\begin{center}
\begin{NiceTabular}{lcccc}[hvlines]
 & V/NV & V/V & NV/NV & NV/V \\
1st open & \cellcolor{tiffanyblue}2 & 3 & 3.5 & \cellcolor{lime}4 \\
2nd open & \cellcolor{tiffanyblue}2 & good 3 & 3 & good 4 \\
3rd open & \cellcolor{tiffanyblue}2.5 & 3.5* & \cellcolor{lime}4* & \cellcolor{lime}5* \\
overcall & \cellcolor{tiffanyblue}2 & 3* & \cellcolor{lime}good 4* & \cellcolor{lime}4.5* \\
overcall (partner PH) & \cellcolor{tiffanyblue}2.5 & 3.5* & \cellcolor{lime}4* & \cellcolor{lime}5* \\
\end{NiceTabular}
\end{center}

The color indicates number of cards promised. Normally, our 2-level and 3-level promises 6-cards, but:
\begin{itemize}
    \setlength{\itemsep}{0pt}
    \item {\color{green}green} means frequently 5-card at 2-level
    \item {\color{tiffanyblue}blue} means promises 7+ cards at 3-level
\end{itemize}

The markers indicates the "wilderness" of the range:
\begin{itemize}
    \setlength{\itemsep}{0pt}
    \item *star means that we'll preempt even wilder when holding little-to-none defensive values. 5332 with 0pts probably justifies for 5*
    \item "good" means that we should bid more cautiously, requiring a bit more shape or less defensiveness
    \item no matter with or w/o markers, defensive values are always a discourage signal when preempting
\end{itemize}

To summarize the table:
\begin{itemize}
    \setlength{\itemsep}{0pt}
    \item Always sound when unfavorable
    \item We roughly follow the 2-3-4 rule in the first seat, while slightly stretching NV/NV a bit
    \item 2nd seat is more conservative
    \item In contrast, when partner PH, we should be super aggressive as modern style suggested
    \item Overcall with an unpassed partner is in the middle-ground of 1st and 3rd seat preempt
    \item When NV, 2-level frequently allows 5-card for 3rd seat or overcall
\end{itemize}

\subsection{subseq}

If we faced a wider range preemption from partner, depending on auction, but we should generally raise with a bit more offensive values.
For example, I would just treat partner's \texttt{{P - (1m) - 2S}} at NV/NV as something like \texttt{KJxxx.x.Kxx.xxxx}, therefore probably only raise with 3-card when unbalanced, or with a very weak hand.
After all, the disuption has already been made, and further preempt's gain is not as worth.
