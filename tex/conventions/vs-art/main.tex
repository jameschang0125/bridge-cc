\section{vs artificial bids}

To tackle those artificial bids' problem, we need classify them into:

\begin{itemize}
    \setlength\itemsep{0pt}
    \item transfers: promising only 1 known (5+ cards) suit
    \item multi: promise 1 unknown suit
    \item assumed-fit: promises 0 or 2+ suits: take-out, Micheals, unusual, fertilizer, etc.    
\end{itemize}

Situations can also be classified into:

\begin{itemize}
    \setlength\itemsep{0pt}
    \item opponents opened pre (or non-pre, ex: Flannery or precision 2D)
    \item we opened and are overcalled (since in this case partner has promised some strength)
\end{itemize}

\subsection{advancer's suit and NT bids}

General principles are:

\begin{itemize}
    \setlength\itemsep{0pt}
    \item advances are generally natrual if it's not opponents suit
    \item opponents 5+ card suit usually treated as cuebid (meaning might vary)
    \item if we opened NT, then transfer Leb applied
    \item P then bid shows a weaker hand
\end{itemize}
